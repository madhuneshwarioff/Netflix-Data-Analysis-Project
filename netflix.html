Project Showcase Netflix Data Analysis

Streaming Catalog Story

Studying how Netflix content grows, shifts, and spreads across the world.

In this project, I analyzed Netflix's content catalog to understand how movies and TV shows are distributed by year, genre, country, and content rating. I used Python for cleaning and exploratory analysis, then built an interactive Power BI dashboard to present the patterns clearly.

What I Did

From raw content data to a business-ready dashboard

01

Prepared the dataset

I cleaned and organized the Netflix titles data so it could be explored consistently across years, countries, types, and ratings.

02

Performed exploratory analysis

I used Python and Pandas to study the structure of the content library and check how movies and shows are distributed across different dimensions.

03

Visualized content patterns

With Matplotlib, I explored genre frequency, rating patterns, production trends, and country contribution to understand what stands out in the catalog.

04

Built the Power BI dashboard

Key Insights

What I found in the data

Movies dominate the catalog

Movies make up a larger share of Netflix's content library than TV shows.

Growth accelerated after 2016

The content library shows a noticeable increase in production and additions from around 2016 onward.

The United States leads title count

Country-level analysis showed the United States contributing the highest number of titles.

Drama and international genres stand out

Genre analysis suggests that drama and internationally oriented content appear frequently across the catalog.

Dashboard Preview

A clearer view of Netflix content behavior

The dashboard brings together title counts, type split, time-based growth, country contribution, content ratings, and genre distribution in one interface with interactive filters.
